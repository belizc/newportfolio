My Story

Growing up I suffered from selective mutism, and found it difficult to connect to other people. I had a strong propensity for telling the truth, often to my own detriment, and reading. Truth-telling and escapism proved counterproductive to social endeavours, but great for academic achievement. Once in sixth grade I received praise from teachers and classmates alike for a portrait of a “Diana Monkey” I had drawn. When a fellow classmate lauded about how “dope” I was I angrily responded “YOU’RE DOPE!” in tears, having perceived the compliment as a slight.

As an avid reader I was admitted to the top ranked school in Michigan (City High/Middle School), where I was encouraged to apply to college. To better understand the world around me I decided to study literature and history via a liberal studies degree at Grand Valley State University (GVSU). However, as my education became more self-directed I struggled to keep up. I struggled through social blunders galore, disorganization, an impaired sense of time, and poor working memory. Working with a licensed neuropsychologist I was diagnosed with Autism Spectrum Disorder, and was connected to great resources.

As a Latina with diagnosed Autism Spectrum Disorder I hope to leverage my data science background to advocate for social good.
